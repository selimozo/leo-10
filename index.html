In the 2004/05 season, Lionel Messi made his official debut for FC Barcelona. At 17 years old, he played his first La Liga match on October 16, 2004, against Espanyol. Later in the season, on May 1, 2005, Messi scored his first senior goal for the club against Albacete, becoming the youngest scorer in Barcelona's history at the time. This season marked the start of Messi's professional career.

In the 2011/12 season, Lionel Messi had a record-breaking year with FC Barcelona. Messi has set the world record for a goals in a single calendar year, with a grand total of 91, beating the previous all-time best set in 1972 by Germany and Bayern’s Gert Müller, who got 85. Key moments of the season included his impressive performances in La Liga, where he scored multiple goals in games, scored twice vs Real Madrid (one was in the 88th minute) and won 3-2 that game and won the Spanish Super Cup. Messi also scored in Copa del Rey final, leading Barcelona to victory over Athletic Bilbao. Messi also scored FIVE goals in a 7-1 win over Bayer Leverkusen in the UEFA Champions League (record in UCL). Messi's individual brilliance was a highlight of the season. His extraordinary goal tally and performances earned him the Ballon d'Or for the fourth time.
